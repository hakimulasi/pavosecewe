Ever wondered what the Channel 7 voice over guy looks like?

Publish date: 2024-07-16

HAVE you ever been watching a show on Channel 7 and asked yourself, “who is the guy doing the voice over?”

If the answer’s yes, get ready to find out (If the answer’s no, do me a favour and just keep reading anyway).

It turns out his name is Matt Wills and he’s been the voice behind the promo for almost every single Channel 7 show (other than the news) for more than 10 years.

“I’m in there every day, Monday to Friday,” said Wills, who uses the drive on the way to the Channel 7 studio in Pyrmont to warm up his voice.

“I’ve got some singing exercises that I do for about 15 or 20 minutes while I’m driving in the car … I think people in traffic might think I’m a bit crazy but it usually works.”

When Wills arrives, he’s handed a pile of scripts ... sometimes half a dozen, sometimes up to 50.

“The most fun ones are the ones where you have to be scary, like Criminal Minds,” said Wills.

“It might take five minutes to voice a 30 second promo or it might take one minute if we get it right first time. For something like Criminal Minds, I’ll record a 30 second promo, a 20 second promo and a 10 second promo so it might take me 10 minutes to do it all.”

On most days, Wills spends about two hours in the recording booth with his work typically airing on TV a week later.

This means that he gets a sneak peek at some of the upcoming plot lines of Channel 7 shows which people “always” try to coax out of him.

“Most of the time I tell them they’ll just have to watch to find out,” said Wills.

“I have a niece who was at one time very obsessed with Home and Away and always wanted to know what was happening next”.

This constant hounding about popular TV shows is something that Wills and voice over artists from other TV networks often joke about when they catch up.

“Most of us are friends,” said Wills.

“We don’t have voiceover get-togethers as such but we often bump into each other. We’re all very nice and polite to one another because you never know when you might need that friendship,” he said while laughing.

Despite the fact most of us hear his voice almost every single day, Wills is completely unknown by the Australian public. He’s always heard but never seen.

“In all this time I think only one person has out of the blue said to me, ‘Gee, I recognise your voice, are you the guy from Channel 7?’ said Wills.

Sinan Akçıl is a Turkish singer, songwriter, composer, actor, model, and record producer. He is best known as a producer for the song Düm Tek Tek which was Turkey’s entry to the 2009 Eurovision Song Contest. It was performed by Hadise Açıkgöz and garnered 177 points thus winning 4th place in the finals.

    Sinan Akçıl Facts

  • He comes from a family of musicians.
  • His parents are divorced.
  • His career as a composer started in 2007 when he worked on Emel Müftüoğlu’s 8th studio album, Arabesk. The success led him to work with singer Zeynep Cassalini.
  • Sinan has worked with artists such as Ferhat Göçer, Mustafa Sandal, Gülben Ergen, Ziynet Sali, and Demet Akalin.
  • He released 7 studio albums between 2011 and 2020 titled Kalp Sesi (2011), Karnaval (2012), Kapı (2013), Tabi Tabi (2014), Best of Aşk (2015), Yüzyılın Aşkı (2017), and Piyanist (2020).
  • Sinan’s voice has been criticized by several prominent Turkish musicians and singers, including Tolga Akyıldız, Leman Sam, Naim Dilmener, Sema Eryiğit, and Atilla Aydoğdu.
  • He was a judge on the Turkish version of the reality-TV singing competition, The X Factor in 2014. Other judges were Nihat Odabaşı, Atiye Deniz, and Nigar Cemal.
  • Sinan would have liked to work with Ahmet Kaya, a Turkish artist who passed away in 2000.
  • He listens to pop, rap, and Arabesque/Arabesk in his private life.
  • Besides Turkish, Sinan is also fluent in English and French.

  • Sinan works at night and sleeps throughout the day.

Marty Makary- Biography

Marty Makary is a surgeon and an English-American physician. He is a health policy expert at Johns Hopkins. Makary was a prolific author who penned and published numerous novels. The New York Times named him a best-selling novelist. Makary was elected to the National Academy of Medicine in 2018.

Marty Makary’s Professional Career

Marty Makary began his career after graduating from Georgetown University, where he completed a surgical residency. Later, in Washington, D.C., he worked as a writer for the Advisory Board Company. Makary also received sub-specialty surgery training in surgical oncology and gastrointestinal surgery at Johns Hopkins under surgeon John Cameron. He then became a partner in Cameron’s faculty practice.

Makary’s first novel, Mama Maggie, a personal story about his aunt, earned him a Nobel Peace Prize while he was working in Caira’s rubbish slums. Furthermore, in 2018, he published The Price We Pay, a book about how business leaders may reduce their health-care expenditures and the grass-roots campaign to return a medicine to its noble objective. His other books include Unaccountable, General Surgery Review, and others.

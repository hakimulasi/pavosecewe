Who was Adina Azarian? | The US Sun

Publish date: 2024-08-07

TRAGEDY struck when an air pilot reportedly lost control of a private jet and crashed into a nearby area.

The disaster claimed the lives of Adina Azarian and other members of John and Barbara Rumpel's family.

Who was Adina Azarian?

Adina Azarian was a 49-year-old realtor from East Hampton, New York.

She was one of John and Barbara Rumpel's two daughters.

As a realtor, Adina owned her own boutique firm in Manhattan.

Later in her career, she became an agent for the franchise Keller Williams Realty.

While working with Keller Williams, Adina's listings were mainly located in the Hamptons area of Long Island.

Her clients described her as "an incredible asset," who "went above and beyond" for her clientele.

Her customers also reviewed her as "responsive, perceptive, and persistent."

"I am grateful for working with Adina for my purchase in the Hamptons. Entirely because of her, I had a great buying experience." said one buyer.

What happened to Adina?

On June 4, 2023, a private jet transporting Adina from Tennessee to New York crashed in the Shenandoah Valley.

Following the tragedy, Adina, her two-year-old daughter, and the family's nanny were revealed to be one of the passengers who died in the disaster.

At the time, the three were going home after a four-day visit to John and Barbara Rumpel's residence in North Carolina.

"It descended at 20,000 feet a minute, and nobody could survive a crash from that speed," the grieving father told The New York Times.

While exact details remain to be discovered, John theorizes "they all just would have gone to sleep and never woke up" if the plane lost pressurization.

Adina was preceded in death by her sister Victoria; she passed away from a scuba diving incident in 1994.

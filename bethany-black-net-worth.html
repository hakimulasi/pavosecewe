FAQs about Bethany Black

Who is Bethany Black?

Bethany Black is an acclaimed English stand-up comedian, actress, and writer known for her dark-tinged comedy. She’s recognized for being the first trans person to play a trans character in a British TV series and the first openly trans actor in Doctor Who.

What are some notable aspects of Black’s career?

She started her comedy career in 2005, reaching the final of Chortle’s Student Comedy Awards and receiving a Best Debut nomination at the Leicester Comedy Festival. Her solo shows cover a range of personal topics, including gender reassignment, alcoholism, and drug addiction.

Has she received any awards or recognitions?

Yes, in 2011, she was ranked 96th in The Independent on Sunday’s Pink List of influential LGBT Britons. Additionally, she won the Best Dramatic Role for Banana at the Transgender Television Awards in 2016.

What are some of her current projects or roles?

Black has performed in various shows, including Cucumber and Banana on Channel 4, and guest-starred as 474 in Doctor Who. She’s also participated in The Chase as a contestant.

How does she incorporate her personal experiences into her comedy?

Bethany’s stand-up is deeply personal, where she shares stories and overshares, making her autism a central part of her act. She discusses how being autistic affects her and her view of the world, offering a unique perspective in comedy.

What challenges has Bethany faced, and how has she addressed them?

Black talks openly about her struggles with understanding social cues, noise sensitivity, and the difficulties of public transport due to unpredictability. Comedy has provided her a space where she feels in control and can communicate on her terms.

How does Bethany Black see autism influencing her comedy?

Being autistic, according to Bethany, affects her thinking and allows her to view the world differently, a key element in comedy. She values the opportunity to examine herself and societal perceptions through her comedic lens.

What advice does Bethany Black offer to autistic individuals interested in stand-up comedy?

Bethany encourages autistic individuals to pursue stand-up comedy if they’re interested. She emphasizes how comedy gave her a sense of belonging and a community where she felt at home.

What societal changes does Bethany Black advocate for to support autistic individuals?

She suggests making societal adjustments such as quieter environments, fewer unexpected sounds, and movements, and more predictable behaviors from others to make public spaces more comfortable for autistic people.

How has Black contributed to the LGBTQ+ and comedy communities?

Aside from her groundbreaking roles on television, Black co-founded Funny’s Funny, a comedy organization aimed at providing opportunities for female comedians, showcasing her commitment to inclusivity and support within the comedy and LGBTQ+ communities.

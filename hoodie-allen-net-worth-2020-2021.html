Career

He and Samuel Obey, who was his childhood friend, had formed a duo: Its name was Steve Markowitz and Obey City. The former worked on vocals while the latter was in production. Their first release was making Waves and Bagels Beats. However, Obey stopped producing.

What followed is that Hoodie started releasing his singles with the help of RJ Ferguson (RJF). His singles at that time included UPENN Girls, and You Are Not a Robot. The warm response motivated him to produce two other mixtapes named Pep Rally and Leap Year.

In 2012 on 29th March, Allen released the debut single of an EP he had named All America. His single was called No Interruptions. The second one was No Faith in Brooklyn

A music video for a song called Cake Boy was released in 2013 on 11th February. Another one was released a week later called Fame Is For Assholes (FIFA). The two were singles to be in his upcoming mixtapes. In 2013, he also released a single, Make It Home, a music video for No Interruption, and an EP titled Americoustic.

The following year, Allen released his debut album titled People Keep Talking. Its lead single was Show Me What You are Made Of. The premiere of the song was the same as that of a music video of Happy Gilmore. It featured Tommy Lee, D-WHY, and Motley Crue. Later, he made his television debut on Good Day Philadelphia. He played an accounting version of the song Show Me What You’re Made Of. The performance was after a short interview he had on 15th May 2014.

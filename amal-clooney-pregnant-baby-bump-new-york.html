Amal Clooney dresses her growing bump in New York in two sensational outfits over two days

Publish date: 2024-08-05

    Amal Clooney has been carrying around twins for the past few months, and certainly knows how to dress her bump in style! The international human rights lawyer stunned in a yellow dress as she made her way to the United Nations on Thursday afternoon on her way to speak out against ISIS at 'The Fight against Impunity for Atrocities: Bringing Da'esh to Justice'. George Clooney’s wife showcased her tiny baby bump in a sixties inspired design, teamed with a matching coat and sunglasses. The previous evening, Amal looked radiant in a pink velvet dress, and clearly keen on keeping to the same colour block, matched with a pink coat. Wearing her long hair down, Amal joined the likes of Princess Eugenie to celebrate International Women’s Day at a charity event in the Big Apple.

    Recently, Amal opened up about her high-profile marriage to George and the positive effects it has had on her career. Talking to BBC News at Six, the 39-year-old explained, "I think if there are more people who now understand what's happening about the Yazidis and ISIS, and if there can be some action that results from that, that can help those clients, then I think it's a really good thing to give that case the extra publicity that it may get."

    Amal made her red carpet bump debut last month at the Cesar Awards in Paris. The mum-to-be glowed wearing a strapless white Versace gown that featured a feathered ombre skirt. George, who was honored with a career award at the event, praised his wife in his speech saying, “To my wife Amal, there isn’t a day that goes by that I’m not proud to be your husband. And I am excited about the years to come, and particularly the months to come. I love you very much.”

    Amal and George married in September 2014 in a wedding covered by HELLO! magazine. Amal wore a custom-made, French lace Oscar de la Renta dress which was embroidered with pearls and diamanté accents and featured a gorgeous lace overlay and off-the-shoulder sleeves. The groom, meanwhile looked dapper in a black tuxedo, which was created by his good friend Giorgio Armani, as part of his Made to Measure collection. "My verdict on being Mr and Mrs Clooney? It feels pretty damn great," George told HELLO! at the time.
